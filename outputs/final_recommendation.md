# Final Business Recommendation

# Executive Summary

A regression analysis was performed to identify the business factors associated with monthly sales across retail stores.

Two models were evaluated:

- Simple Regression Model (Marketing Spend only)
- Multiple Regression Model (Marketing Spend + Operational Variables)

The multiple regression model was selected as the final model because it explains approximately **82.85% of the variation in monthly sales (R² = 0.8285)**, compared to only **16.72% (R² = 0.1672)** for the simple regression model.

---

# 1. Which Factors Appear Most Strongly Associated with Monthly Sales?

The regression results indicate that the following variables have the strongest association with monthly sales:

- Customer Footfall (strong positive association)
- Marketing Spend (positive association)
- Inventory Availability Percentage (positive association)
- Staff Count (positive association)
- Customer Rating (positive association)

These variables have statistically significant coefficients and contribute positively to explaining sales performance.

Average Discount Percentage and Competitor Distance also influence sales but have negative coefficients in the final model.

---

# 2. Which Variables Should Leadership Focus On?

Leadership should prioritize investments in:

## Increase Customer Footfall

Higher customer traffic consistently leads to higher monthly sales.

Possible actions:

- Local marketing campaigns
- Loyalty programs
- Community events
- Digital advertising

---

## Maintain Adequate Inventory

Better inventory availability reduces lost sales opportunities.

Possible actions:

- Improve inventory planning
- Reduce stock-outs
- Increase supply chain efficiency

---

## Invest in Marketing

Marketing spend remains a significant driver of sales growth.

Leadership should continue investing in marketing while monitoring return on investment.

---

## Improve Customer Experience

Higher customer ratings are associated with higher monthly sales.

Possible actions:

- Employee training
- Better customer service
- Faster issue resolution
- Store experience improvements

---

## Optimize Staffing Levels

Stores with appropriate staffing levels tend to perform better, suggesting that workforce planning should remain a management priority.

---

# 3. Which Variables Should Not Be Over-Interpreted?

Average Discount Percentage and Competitor Distance should be interpreted carefully.

Although they show statistical relationships with monthly sales, they may also reflect broader business strategies or local market conditions.

Similarly, the Holiday Flag shows only a marginal effect and should not be considered a primary driver of sales decisions without additional analysis.

---

# 4. Recommended Business Actions

Based on the regression results, the following actions are recommended:

- Increase marketing investment in high-potential stores.
- Focus on initiatives that increase customer footfall.
- Maintain high inventory availability to minimize lost sales.
- Continue improving customer satisfaction through better service.
- Optimize staffing based on expected customer demand instead of reducing workforce.
- Use discount strategies selectively rather than relying on frequent high discounts.

These actions are supported by the variables identified as significant in the multiple regression model.

---

# 5. Risks and Limitations

Leadership should consider several limitations before making business decisions:

- The model only includes variables available in the dataset.
- External factors such as local events, economic conditions, competitor promotions, and seasonality are not included.
- Residual analysis shows that some stores performed much better or worse than predicted, indicating that additional business factors may influence sales.
- The regression model assumes linear relationships between variables.

Therefore, the model should be used as a decision-support tool rather than an exact forecasting system.

---

# 6. Why Regression Shows Association but Not Causation

Regression analysis identifies relationships between variables but does not automatically prove that one variable causes another.

For example:

Higher marketing spend is associated with higher sales, but this does not necessarily mean marketing alone causes the increase. Stores with larger marketing budgets may also have better locations, stronger management, or higher customer demand.

Similarly, stores with higher customer ratings may also benefit from better staffing, inventory management, or local market conditions.

Therefore, regression should be used to identify important business drivers and generate insights, while final decisions should be supported by business knowledge, experimentation, and continuous performance monitoring.

---

# Final Recommendation

The multiple regression model should be adopted as the primary analytical model because it explains approximately **82.85% of the variation in monthly sales** and incorporates multiple operational and customer-related factors.

Leadership should focus on increasing customer footfall, maintaining inventory availability, investing in marketing, improving customer satisfaction, and optimizing staffing levels.

These initiatives are expected to provide the greatest opportunity for improving monthly sales while supporting informed, data-driven business decisions.
